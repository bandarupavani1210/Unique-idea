The Health City Project

A Hackathon Project on Public Health & DevOps

Introduction: Our Project's Mission

Our project addresses four critical public health challenges using a visual diorama as a prototype for real-world solutions. We aimed to apply the core principles of DevOps—collaboration, automation, and continuous improvement—to tackle these issues.

Screen Time: How Much Is Too Much?

Excessive screen time is a growing issue linked to several health problems. Our diorama highlights the negative effects and proposes simple, actionable tips.

  • The Problem: Headaches, eye strain, poor sleep, and stress.
  • Our Solutions:
    • The 20-20-20 Rule
    • Digital Detox
    • Limited Screen Time

Fast Food vs. Homemade Food

This section visually compares the health impacts of fast food and homemade food, emphasizing the importance of mindful eating.

  • Fast Food: High sugar, high fat, obesity, and diabetes.
  • Homemade Food: Provides vitamins, energy, and boosts immunity.

Ambulance Delay: Every Minute Matters

We highlighted how traffic congestion can severely delay emergency services, risking patients' lives during the critical "Golden Hour."

  • The Problem: Delayed ambulances mean patients are in danger.
  • Our Solutions:
    • Better Traffic Management
    • Emergency Lanes
    • More Ambulances

Fake Medicines: Real Danger

This part of our project raises awareness about counterfeit drugs and provides simple safety tips for the public.

  • The Problem: Fake medicines can be harmful and are often difficult to spot.
  • Our Solutions (The 20-20-20 Rule):
    • Check Expiry Date
    • Buy From Trusted Stores
    • Don't Risk Your Health

Applying DevOps Principles

Our diorama wasn't just a static display; it was a prototype. The entire process of creating it demonstrated core DevOps principles.

  • Collaboration: We worked as a team, using version control concepts to manage different parts of the project.
  • Automation: We envisioned how a CI/CD pipeline could automatically deploy updates to software that solves these problems.
  • Continuous Improvement: Our project is a foundation that can be continuously improved with more data and user feedback.
